### 🌿 What I'm building

**[abkhaz-language-open](https://github.com/1977-code/abkhaz-language-open)** — an open course + language-data toolkit for Abkhaz (аҧсуа бызшәа):

- a **reproducible corpus → frequency pipeline** over open corpora, with **CC0** datasets;
- a **CEFR A0–A2** methodology & syllabus, draft textbook chapters, and a guide to the complex Abkhaz verb;
- a bilingual (RU / EN) website and a careful open/closed **rights model** that keeps openly-licensed work separate from copyrighted sources.

### 🕯️ Why it matters

The Northwest Caucasian family has **already lost Ubykh** — its last fluent speaker died in 1992. Abkhaz has roughly 130,000 speakers and very few modern, openly-licensed learning materials. Open, reusable resources are a small hedge against that loss — and a template other minority-language communities can copy.
